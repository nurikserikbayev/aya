The Problem

Move supplies where GPS, maps and signal all fail.

  • The UAE imports ~85% of its food (~$13.2B/yr) and targets 50% local production by 2051 — driving heavy investment in greenhouses, vertical farms and desert orchards.
  • These are exactly where automation breaks: GPS dies under canopy and indoors, and crop rows change shape as plants grow.
  • The summer heat-ban (45–50°C, Jun–Sep) legally idles human field labour for months.
The labour sink
~60%
of per-acre operating cost is manual harvest & handling. Hauling crops, water and tools is the #1 job no one wants in the heat.
Same problem, second market
Frontline resupply is the identical problem — GPS-denied, comms-denied, off-road.
The Solution · Why It's Robust

AYA is the brain, not the hardware.

A map-free, GPS-optional, plain-language navigation AI — a compact vision-language-action model + metric depth — sold as software onto any cheap camera-and-depth chassis.

  • Tell it where to go in plain words → it hauls the load through unmapped, changing, signal-denied terrain.
  • No survey. No waypoints. No route to pre-teach. No remote leash.
  • Runs on a single camera + cheap depth — not a $50k LiDAR/RTK stack.
FLOOR
Vision autonomy. Drives from camera + depth — no map to break, no GPS to lose, no radio to jam. Comms loss is the #1 cause of UGV mission failure; AYA has no link to lose.
DEGRADE
Honest about sensors. Cameras dim in dust & glare — so AYA slows on uncertainty instead of guessing.
SAFE
Safe-stop. When it can't be sure, it stops — safely, every time. Never a blind lunge.

Need guaranteed-coverage routes or centimetre docking? An optional SLAM/Nav2 precision tier layers on top. Map-free by default; precise when you pay for it.
